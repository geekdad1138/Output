Not sure about everyone else, but my LinkedIn feed is full of people retiring from Microsoft. I’m talking people that have been there for decades. People that have seen releases or even worked on things like Xbox, .Net, Bing, Azure, even Windows 95… 

I looked into it, and apparently this year Microsoft is offering voluntary retirement offers.

From GeekWire:
“The program is part of Microsoft’s effort to trim costs and reshape its workforce while pouring tens of billions into AI. For a company that laid off more than 15,000 people last year, with additional cuts possible, a voluntary offer is also a gentler way to thin its ranks.”

I always saw getting a job at Microsoft like getting called up to the major leagues. Making the show, the big time. These people “made it”, gave decades of their lives to Redmond, and now they’re taking the retirement package. I think some are actually retiring, and others are even going on to look for new adventures.

I’ve been playing with Microsoft toys from BASIC in the 80s, to vscode today. I remember being excited at work when Visual Studio first came out. I literally grew up alongside Windows, from 3.11 to NT and XP to 11, including a few Windows CE devices along the way.

It’s surreal to know that some of these new retirees were part of creating the Xbox, or Bing, or .NET.

To all the new retirees, thanks for giving me fun toys to play with! Enjoy your retirement and whatever new adventures you find yourself on!
