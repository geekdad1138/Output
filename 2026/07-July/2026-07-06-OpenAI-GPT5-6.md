GPT 5.6 is coming out soon, and it turns out Mythos isn't the only kid on the block everyone is scared of. The government has asked OpenAI to delay release of the model, just like when mythos/fable was pulled back.

From the article over at Cyber Security News:
"OpenAI has agreed to stagger the public release of its latest AI model, GPT-5.6, after the Trump administration formally requested the company limit initial access to a select group of government-approved partners, citing the model’s advanced capabilities and national security implications."

GPT 5.6 is supposed to have equal capabilities to Mythos, in the ever competing race between the AI companies to make the biggest best tools on the block.

As these situations keep coming up, a big question keeps getting posed... who is in charge? Is there a government entity reviewing these? What about international models made outside of the US?

We're definitely going to be seeing more of this through the years as more models come up, and these questions need to be considered for the future.
