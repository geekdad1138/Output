Is anyone else excited about the July 1 token payday with GitHub Copilot? When GHC switched their billing in June, we all had to tighten our belts, start learning how to write efficient prompts, use less expensive models, and watch our budgets to make sure we didn't hit our cap by June 30.

Some people didn't put a cap on their budgets and just ran with it, racking up some high prices.

From an article over at TechTimes, 
"agentic developers who spent June running autonomous coding sessions on frontier models report costs estimated at 10x to 50x higher than what their flat subscriptions once cost.
Developers projected bills jumping from $29 to $750 and from $50 to $3,000"

- On the bad side, the glory days are over where you had a small monthly bill and almost never ran out of tokens.

- On the good side, developers are learning more about token consumption, optimizing the requests we send to AI models, reserving the expensive AI models for the difficult tasks, using plan/ask modes instead of full agent mode, and just generally being aware that tokens cost money.

Turns out AI is expensive, and we're just now seeing the bill.
