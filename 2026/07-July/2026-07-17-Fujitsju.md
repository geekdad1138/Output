"Physical AI is the next industrial revolution — and it will be made in Japan." That's the quote from NVIDIA's Jensen Huang, when he met with Fujitsu and the top robotics manufacturers in Japan, like FANUC.

CEO of FANUC CORPORATION Kenji Yamaguchi said this of the collaboration:
"Through this collaboration and by leveraging the AI technologies of NVIDIA, a strategic partner of both companies, we will strive to create a society in which people and robots coexist and collaborate seamlessly, strengthen industrial competitiveness, create new value, and contribute to the realization of a sustainable society."

Japan's robotics manufacturing companies have always led the way in automation, and FANUC has always had a close relationship with Fujitsu since FANUC's founder Dr. Inaba worked at Fujitsu developing numerical controls in the 1950s before starting FANUC in 1972.

The advancements in the field of AI are developing in orders of magnitudes faster than the industrial revolution, and adoption is far faster than the internet's adoption.

By establishing collaborations with top manufacturers and AI technology producers, Jensen's statement is a pretty safe bet.
