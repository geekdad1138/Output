Millions of hectares of forest are burning in Canada, filling the midwest skies with smoke. This is not a dystopian sci-fi book, this is reality.

AI gets a lot of bad press for the environmental impact of data centers, but today people are using AI to help fight the wildfires.

At the University of Toronto, they are working on a new technology called TankerVision, to create more accurate AI-powered fire prediction models.

Professor Steven Lake Waslander from U of T said:
"Our big idea here is to use AI and computer vision to capture unprecedented numbers of fires from the air to build more sophisticated, data-driven wildfire prediction models."
