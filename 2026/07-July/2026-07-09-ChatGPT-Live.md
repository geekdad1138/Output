ChatGPT is getting a little better at chatting now, throwing in responses like "uh-huh, yeah, ok" just like a real person listening to you with their new "GPT-Live-1".

From TechCrunch.com
"OpenAI today released new conversational models, called GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini, claiming that they sound more natural and can handle turn-taking better. These are full-duplex models, meaning they can speak and listen at the same time, allowing users to interrupt naturally and enabling features like live translation."

Seems like almost every AI company is working on upgrading their voice chats. Gemini just recently released a bunch of new voices, Amazon's Alexa is all fresh and new, and Apple's Siri will be seeing some upgrades soon too.

If we really want to make the AIs more human we might have to program in a "hey, are you actually listening to me or just saying uh-huh?" mode.
