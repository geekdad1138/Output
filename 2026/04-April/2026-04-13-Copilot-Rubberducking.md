Copilot now has AI rubberducking!

If you haven’t heard of that term, it’s a way for coders to find problems by explaining their code to an imaginary rubber duck. 

If you’ve ever gone to a fellow programmer and started to explain your problem to them only to figure it out yourself half way through explaining it.. that’s a rubberducking success story.

As far as Copilot, the GitHub Copilot CLI now has a feature where you can have one AI rubberduck another one. You can use Claude Opus 4.6 to plan your development, and before executing it, have GPT 5.4 critique the plan.

Rajeev Pentyala has a great write up with screenshots and step by step instructions for how to do it.
