A medical imaging AI tool was found to not be trained enough on pediatric data. That matters a lot when it comes to medical diagnosis.

Out of over 500,000 images, only about 6,000 involved children.

In AI we know training data source matters. We have to be careful and intentional about what data we feed models, or at least declare what the proficiencies and shortcomings are for any AI tool.

From the article:
“Children account for about 22% of the U.S. population but new study finds they make up less than 2% of the public medical imaging datasets used to build tools that help radiologists read scans”
