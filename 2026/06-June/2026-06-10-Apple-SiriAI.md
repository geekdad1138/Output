Apple announced Siri AI at WWDC, as a big upgrade for their digital assistant. The position they’re taking is that they are focusing on utility and privacy for users.

In the words of Apple’s senior VP Craig Federighi: “We believe that truly helpful AI must be centered around you and your needs."

To catch up in this space, Apple teamed up with Google to use Gemini as the back end for their AI rollout.

Siri was one of the first well built digital assistants that was so famous and lifelike it was getting used as a comedy bit in shows like Big Bang Theory. But then has taken a back seat to the newer LLM releases.

Apple’s massive and loyal customer base in the mobile space gives them a good chance to catch up, but it will be interesting to see how the new Siri AI stacks up to the rapidly evolving AI models.
