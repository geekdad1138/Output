OpenAI is teaming up with Broadcom and Celestica to make Jalapeño chips to make ChatGPT run faster. I feel like there's a good Dad joke there somewhere.

Jalapeño isn't a flavored nacho chip, it's an ASIC which apparently stands for "Aplication Specific Integrated Circuit".

Long story short, it's a super special hardware made specifically for ChatGPT to get the job done faster, without so much reliance on NVIDIA data centers.

The main function of the new chip is "inference". I asked my AI buddy Gemini to simplify this one, and it said:

"In the AI world, the lifecycle of a model is split cleanly into two completely distinct phases: Training and Inference.
To put it simply: Training is going to school. Inference is taking the test."

So basically the day to day operations of the LLMs where it is running through the pre-trained models, will all soon be running on these spicy new Jalapeño chips.

Broadcom and Celestica are teaming up to do the hardware work.
From the article over at Yahoo:
"Broadcom provided chip implementation, networking capabilities, and silicon manufacturing, while Celestica contributed to board and rack system integration, networking, and scalable production system development."

Apple is pushing for more on-device AI with Siri, Microsoft just released low-token usage MAI models, China is putting their data centers underwater, Bezos wants to put them in space, and now OpenAI is making their own chips to join up with Google's TPUs and Amazon's Titanium.

Everyone is running the same race, trying to minimize data center use, and along the way pushing the envelope for new technology to process all of our needs.
