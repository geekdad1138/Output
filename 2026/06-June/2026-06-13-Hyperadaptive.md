Just finished reading hashtag#Hyperadaptive by [Melissa M. Reeve](https://www.linkedin.com/in/melissamreeve/). Insightful, visionary, instructional, transformational, incredible!

There are countless books, websites, articles and consultants out there telling you what you should and shouldn't do with your AI transformation. This book takes the time to walk you through the five stages to actually do it step by step, along with example after example of companies that have had successes and failures trying it.

It helps that right off the bat Melissa talks about her time in Japan studying the actual Toyota Production System. (I'm very jealous) She's not just someone who had some good ideas, she's been working in the industry and understands it to it's core.

I think my only complaint is that I'm impatient. I don't want to have to go through stages 1-4 to get to the successes of stage 5, but she provides the path, the blueprint, the instruction set to get there.

At the end of the transformation, the hyperadaptive company she describes seems less like a strict business model with silos, KPIs and ROIs. Instead it sounds more like a living breathing organism with a brain fully integrated to provide AI native functionality, build value streams, and change the way we think about work.

Can't recommend the book enough, whether you are considering an AI transformation for your company, or just a big nerd like me fascinated by the way AI can change our world for the better.
