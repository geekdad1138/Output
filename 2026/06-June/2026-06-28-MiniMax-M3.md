The open source community got a big boost this week, as the Shanghai based company MiniMax published the model weights for their latest M3 model to the public through hugging face.

They followed it up with a publication to arXiv about their new “Sparse Attention” architecture, and how their open weight model is keeping up with most of the AIs from private companies. 

From the article at tech times:
“MSA solves this through a two-branch design that avoids computing attention across the full sequence. A lightweight Index Branch scans the key-value (KV) cache — the stored representations of all previous tokens — and identifies the most relevant blocks for a given query. A heavier Main Branch then runs exact softmax attention, but only over those selected blocks. The rest of the context is skipped entirely.”

Now I’m not going to lie and say I understood every bit of what that means. But like a good AI enthusiast I went off and chatted with AI to learn more about it.

Thinking of it like an encyclopedia, the old way for AIs to find the right information was to read every word of every page to look for the right answer.

The new MSA way is to skip to the index, find the most likely relevant sections, then read those in full.

Sounds just like what a human would do, right?
It’s funny to see so many AI solutions coming up with better and faster results that keep mirroring human brain behavior.
