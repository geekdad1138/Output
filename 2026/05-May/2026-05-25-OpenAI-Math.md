OpenAI just had a Good Will Hunting moment, making a breakthrough on an 80 year old math problem posed in 1946.

The question posed by Paul Erdős:
“If you take a sheet of paper and add some dots, how many pairs can be the same distance apart?”

OpenAI’s model didn’t solve the problem outright, but it did disprove other beliefs about how to find the answer.

The AI work was validated by top mathematicians Thomas Bloom, Tim Gowers, and Andrew Rogoyski.

Rogoyski said about the findings:
“It’s becoming clear that AI is impacting the world of creative thought and will become a fundamental tool of future scientific research,” he said.
