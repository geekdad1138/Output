MIT hooked up AI to themselves with electrodes and let it move their arms and hands. Integrated camera from glasses they wore. This is some serious Ghost in the Shell energy.

Peter He, Ashley Neall, Valdemar Danry, Daniel Kaijzer, Yutong Wu, and Sean Lewis teamed up for the MIT Hackathon 2026.

Incredibly they made this prototype in 48 hours.
They hooked up Claude to the system and told it to move their human body.
The possibilities for this to be used for good are endless.
But right now it's just... blowing my mind that we hooked up an AI to control someone's body.
