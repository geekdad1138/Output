Gartner just dropped a new Magic Quadrant for "Enterprise AI Coding Agents". In the Leaders quadrant? GitHub, Anthropic, Cursor, and OpenAI.

The other quadrant scores are:
- Challengers: Amazon Web Services, Google, Alibaba Cloud, Cognition
- Niche Players: BytePlus, JetBrains, Atlassian
- Visionaries: Tabnine

Each of the vendors has a detailed breakdown on strengths and cautions, in case you haven't started religious wars over your favorite IDE yet.

Their "Strategic Planning Assumptions" section is pretty wild:

From the report:
- By 2027, over 65% of engineering teams using agentic coding will treat IDEs as optional, shifting control, governance and validation to automated platforms.
- By 2028, more than 70% of enterprise software engineers will rely on AI coding agents for both synchronous and asynchronous development tasks.
- By 2028, asynchronous AI coding agent workflows will improve software engineering team productivity by 30% to 50%, surpassing the 0% to 20% gains from AI code assistants in 2025.
- By 2028, AI coding costs will overtake the average developer’s salary due to rising LLM token consumption and increased consumption-based licensing cost.

Just think of that... in less than a year over half of teams are going to go from opening the IDE to do AI Paired programming, to completely using automated platforms.

And then the other big prediction, is that the end game of this is a rise in token costs so high it will cost more than a developer's salary!
