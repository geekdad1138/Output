Microsoft and EY are investing over $1B on a new initiative for corporate AI adoption rollouts, with their Microsoft's FDE AI-native Hypervelocity Engineers.

Not going to lie here, "FDE AI-native Hypervelocity Engineer" would be a pretty cool job title.

This is all part of Microsoft's "Frontier Firms" program, and their "Forward Deployed Engineers (FDE)".

EY went through the program first as "Client Zero" and did things like create intelligent copilot agents, modernize operations with power platform, 
create a multi-agent framework to help with auditing, and use azure AI document intelligence.

From the article:
"Clients will be empowered to become Frontier Firms that scale and generate value, through workforce upskilling, embedded change management and continuous optimization of agentic AI transformation."

Janet Truncale, EY Global Chair and CEO:
"Together with Microsoft, EY is supporting clients to unlock value through rapid deployment of AI at scale."

[Judson Althoff](https://www.linkedin.com/in/judsonalthoff/), CEO of Microsoft's Commercial Business:
"AI is quickly moving from experimentation to a core driver of business performance, and the companies pulling ahead are those scaling AI Transformation."
