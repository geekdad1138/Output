Just finished reading Open to Work: How to Get Ahead in the Age of AI, by Ryan Roslansky, LinkedIn CEO and Microsoft EVP, and Aneesh Raman, LinkedIn’s Chief Economic Opportunity Officer.

It's a quick read and does a good job of highlighting not only the ways you can be successful with AI, it also helps outline the things that make us special, individual, and unique.

A lot of the book talks about being open to work, how to get a job, etc. which should be no surprise since the book is called Open to Work and was written by LinkedIn executives. But even if you're not changing jobs, it has a lot of valuable advice about self-discovery, skills idenfication, and of course the 5 Cs: Curiosity, Courage, Creativity, Compassion, and Communication.

Whether you're looking for a job, worried that AI is going to take your job, or just trying to figure out what your place is in the AI enabled workplace, it's worth the read.
